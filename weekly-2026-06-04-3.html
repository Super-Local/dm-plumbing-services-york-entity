Common Plumber Problems and Quick Fixes

Plumbing issues have a habit of appearing at the most inconvenient moments. Whether it's a dripping tap keeping you awake at night or a boiler that's stopped working on the coldest morning of the year, knowing how to identify common problems — and what you can do about them before calling a professional — can save both time and money.

Dripping Taps

A dripping tap is one of the most frequently reported household plumbing problems. In most cases, the culprit is a worn or damaged washer. Over time, washers degrade due to the friction and pressure they experience each time the tap is used. Replacing a washer is a relatively straightforward task — turn off the water supply, remove the tap head, and swap out the old washer for a new one of the same size. If the dripping continues after replacing the washer, the tap seat itself may be damaged and will need professional attention.

Low Water Pressure

Weak water pressure can make everyday tasks like showering or washing up frustrating. Common causes include a build-up of limescale in the pipes or showerhead, a partially closed stopcock, or a leak somewhere in the system. Start by unscrewing the showerhead and soaking it in descaling solution overnight. If the problem persists throughout the entire property, check that the main stopcock is fully open. Persistent low pressure across multiple outlets often points to a deeper issue within the pipework that requires a qualified plumber to diagnose.

Blocked Drains

Blocked drains are another common complaint, typically caused by a build-up of hair, grease, soap residue, or food debris. For minor blockages, a plunger can often do the job effectively. Alternatively, pouring a mixture of bicarbonate of soda and white vinegar down the drain, followed by hot water, can help break down softer blockages. Avoid relying on chemical drain cleaners too frequently, as these can corrode older pipes over time. If blockages keep recurring, there may be a more significant obstruction further along the drainage system that needs professional clearing.

Running Toilets

A toilet that continues to run after flushing is not only annoying but can significantly increase your water bill. The most common cause is a faulty flapper valve inside the cistern, which prevents water from sealing properly. Replacing a flapper valve is inexpensive and can usually be done without specialist tools. If the fill valve is the issue, it may need adjusting or replacing entirely. A quick inspection inside the cistern will usually reveal which component is misbehaving.

When to Call a Professional

While many minor plumbing problems can be managed with a bit of patience and the right guidance, there are situations where attempting a DIY fix can make things considerably worse. Burst pipes, gas-related issues, boiler faults, and extensive leaks should always be handled by a qualified plumber. Acting quickly and seeking professional help at the right moment can prevent minor inconveniences from turning into costly repairs.
