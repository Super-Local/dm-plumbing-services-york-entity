Common Plumber Problems and How to Solve Them

Plumbing issues have a habit of appearing at the most inconvenient times, and knowing what you're dealing with can make all the difference. Whether it's a dripping tap keeping you awake or a boiler that won't fire up, understanding the common causes helps you act quickly and call in the right help. DM Plumbing Services in York handles these kinds of problems day in and day out, and this guide covers the five most frequent issues and how a professional plumber approaches them.

1. Dripping Taps and Leaking Pipes

A dripping tap is one of the most reported plumbing complaints in the UK. It's easy to ignore, but a single dripping tap can waste a significant amount of water over time and push up your utility bills. The cause is usually a worn washer, O-ring, or ceramic disc inside the tap cartridge. A plumber will isolate the water supply, disassemble the tap, replace the faulty component, and reassemble everything — often a straightforward fix that takes under an hour.

Leaking pipes are a different matter and require prompt attention. Leaks can stem from corrosion, loose fittings, or high water pressure. A professional will identify the source, determine whether a section of pipe needs replacing or a fitting simply needs re-sealing, and carry out the repair using appropriate materials to ensure a lasting result rather than a temporary patch.

2. Blocked Drains and Toilets

Blocked drains are another everyday headache for homeowners. Kitchen drains often become clogged with grease, food debris, and soap build-up, while bathroom drains tend to block with hair and product residue. A professional plumber will use specialist drain rods, high-pressure jetting equipment, or an auger to clear the blockage thoroughly rather than just shifting it further along the pipe.

Blocked toilets often result from flushing unsuitable items such as wipes, cotton wool, or excessive tissue. If a standard plunger isn't solving the problem, a plumber can use a toilet auger to break up the obstruction safely without damaging the pan or the waste pipework beneath it.

If blockages are recurring in the same drain, a plumber may recommend a CCTV drain survey to identify any underlying structural issues within the pipework that are contributing to the problem.

3. Low Water Pressure

Low water pressure is frustrating, particularly in showers and kitchen taps. It can be caused by a partially closed stopcock, a build-up of limescale in pipes or fittings, a faulty pressure reducing valve, or a problem with the water supply itself. A plumber will systematically check each potential cause, starting with the most accessible components, before recommending the appropriate fix — which might be as simple as descaling a showerhead or as involved as replacing a valve.

4. Boiler and Heating Issues

A boiler that loses pressure, makes unusual noises, or fails to provide consistent heat is one of the most disruptive plumbing problems, especially during colder months. Common culprits include a leak somewhere in the system causing pressure loss, a faulty pressure relief valve, or a build-up of sludge in the radiators reducing heat output. Power flushing the central heating system or fitting a magnetic filter can resolve sludge-related issues and improve overall efficiency.

For boiler faults specifically, a Gas Safe registered engineer should always be consulted. DM Plumbing Services provides reliable heating and plumbing support across York, helping residents get their systems back up and running without delay.

5. Running Toilets and Faulty Cisterns

A toilet that runs continuously is both wasteful and irritating. The problem is almost always internal — a worn flap valve, a faulty float, or a fill valve that isn't shutting off correctly. A plumber will open the cistern, diagnose the exact fault, and replace the relevant component. In most cases, the repair takes less than an hour and immediately resolves the constant water flow.

If your cistern is older or has multiple worn parts, it can sometimes be more cost-effective to replace the internal mechanism entirely rather than carrying out repeated individual repairs.
