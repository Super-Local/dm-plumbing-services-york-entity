Common Plumber Problems and How to Solve Them

Plumbing issues have a habit of appearing at the most inconvenient times, from a dripping tap keeping you awake at night to a boiler that stops working on the coldest morning of the year. Understanding the most common problems — and how a qualified plumber approaches them — can help York homeowners act quickly and avoid costly damage. 1. Dripping Taps and Leaking Pipes

A dripping tap might seem trivial, but left unattended it wastes a surprising amount of water and can cause staining or even minor water damage over time. The most common cause is a worn washer or O-ring inside the tap mechanism. A plumber will isolate the water supply, disassemble the tap head, replace the faulty washer or cartridge, and reassemble the unit — a straightforward repair that immediately stops the drip.

Leaking pipes present a more urgent concern. Pinhole leaks can develop in older copper pipework due to corrosion, while push-fit joints may work loose over time. A professional will locate the source, cut out the damaged section or reseat the joint, and test the repair under pressure before signing off the work. Temporary pipe repair tape is never a long-term solution, which is why calling a tradesperson at the first sign of moisture is always advisable.

2. Blocked Drains and Toilets

Blocked drains are one of the most frequent calls a plumber receives. Kitchen sinks accumulate grease and food debris, while bathroom drains gather hair and soap scum. For toilets, the usual culprit is an inappropriate item being flushed. A plumber will use drain rods, a plunger, or a high-pressure water jetter to clear the blockage safely, without damaging the pipework. Regular drain maintenance is the best way to prevent reoccurrence.

If blockages are becoming a recurring issue in your property, it may be worth having the drain surveyed with a CCTV camera. This can reveal root ingress, collapsed sections, or persistent build-up that requires a longer-term remedy rather than a repeated quick fix.

3. Low Water Pressure

Poor water pressure makes everyday tasks frustrating — showers feel weak, and appliances like dishwashers may not fill correctly. The cause could be a partially closed stopcock, a faulty pressure-reducing valve, or limescale build-up within the pipework, especially in hard-water areas. A plumber will systematically check each potential cause and either flush the system, replace the valve, or fit a booster pump where appropriate.

4. Running Toilets and Faulty Cisterns

A toilet that continues to run after flushing is wasting water continuously. The problem usually lies with a worn flapper valve or a float arm set at the wrong height. Both are inexpensive parts to replace and a plumber can have a cistern running correctly again in a short visit. Ignoring the issue, however, can add significantly to your water bill over several months.

5. Boiler and Hot Water Faults

Loss of hot water or heating is one of the most disruptive plumbing issues a household can face. Common causes include low boiler pressure, a faulty thermostat, an airlock in the system, or a failed diverter valve. A Gas Safe registered engineer will diagnose the fault, repressurise the system if needed, bleed radiators, or arrange parts replacement. Attempting boiler repairs without the correct qualifications is both dangerous and illegal.
