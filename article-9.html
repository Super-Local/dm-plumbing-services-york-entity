Warning Signs You Should Never Ignore

One of the most obvious signs that something is wrong is a persistent drop in water pressure. If your shower or taps suddenly feel weaker than usual, there could be a hidden leak, a blocked pipe, or an issue with the mains supply. Occasional fluctuations are normal, but a sustained reduction in pressure warrants a closer look from a qualified plumber.

Unusual sounds coming from your pipes are another red flag. Banging, gurgling, or whistling noises can indicate trapped air, loose fittings, or a more serious blockage building up within the system. These sounds are often the first indication that something is not functioning as it should, and ignoring them can lead to pipe damage over time.

Slow-draining sinks, baths, or showers are often dismissed as minor inconveniences, but they frequently point to a partial blockage that will only worsen. Recurring blockages that keep coming back after DIY attempts are a clear sign that a professional assessment is needed to identify the root cause rather than simply treating the symptom.

Visible Damage and Damp Patches

If you notice damp patches on walls, ceilings, or floors without an obvious explanation, there is a good chance you have a hidden leak somewhere within your plumbing system. Water damage spreads quickly and can compromise the structural integrity of a property, encourage mould growth, and create hazardous conditions. In these situations, acting promptly is essential.

Discoloured water coming from your taps is another concern that should not be overlooked. Brown or rust-coloured water can suggest corroding pipes, whilst cloudy or milky water may indicate an issue with your water supply or boiler. Either way, it is not something you should continue using without having it inspected by a professional.
